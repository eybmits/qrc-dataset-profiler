The practical question

QRC is not treated as universally better. The benchmark asks where it is empirically worth testing under a frozen, fair protocol.

Dataset first

Every time series is summarized by 30 measured properties before the model decision is made.

Frozen comparison

Three globally calibrated QRC variants are compared against a globally calibrated, feature-matched sparse leaky ESN.

Regime map

The output is not a universal win claim. It is a map of where QRC evidence should be concentrated next.

Researcher triage tool

The public site does not upload private time series to a hosted backend. Researchers run the analyzer locally, paste or upload a CSV in their browser, and get a plain-language QRC priority signal.

A positive recommendation means "QRC is worth testing next." It does not prove quantum advantage on the submitted dataset.

What the atlas says

  • QRC is selective, not broadly dominant. ESN remains the stronger default on average.
  • QRC-useful cases are structured. Useful pockets recur across discovery and validation splits.
  • Slow, stateful forecasting is the clearest current marker. Drifting trends, regime changes, persistent low-frequency structure, long memory, and correlated fluctuations are the most interpretable high-priority region.
  • Dataset-level triage is practical. Researchers can profile a new time series before spending effort on QRC evaluation.

What it does not claim

  • No universal quantum advantage. The title uses "advantage" in the empirical benchmark sense: lower forecasting error under the declared frozen protocol.
  • No mechanism proof yet. The atlas identifies where QRC helps; it does not prove whether the mechanism is coupling, dissipation, interference, or broader reservoir memory.
  • No cloud diagnosis of private data. The public page documents the analyzer, but researchers run their CSV locally.
